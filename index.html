About

I am a PhD student at the Faculty of Linguistics, University of Oxford and a researcher at the Center for Information and Language Processing, LMU Munich. I am co-advised by Janet Pierrehumbert and Hinrich Schütze. My PhD is funded by the AHRC and the German Academic Scholarship Foundation. I am currently visiting the Stanford NLP Group, where I am working with Dan Jurafsky. Earlier this year, I was interning in the Language Team of DeepMind, where I was working with Angeliki Lazaridou, Elena Gribovskaya, Jordan Hoffmann, and Tom McGrath.

My research focuses on the link between natural language processing and the social and cognitive sciences. I am particularly passionate about building methods to accommodate extralinguistic factors of text data (such as time, geography, ideology) and improving tokenization for language models.
